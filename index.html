TAP: Task-Agnostic Pretraining for Data-Efficient Vision-Language-Action Models

First Author*, Second Author*, Third Author

arXiv Preprint, 2025
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

TAP unlocks "free" task-agnostic interaction data (e.g., exploration and play) for pretraining VLA policies, then finetunes with only a handful of expert demonstrations — matching baselines that use orders of magnitude more expert data.

Abstract

Modern Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models such as OpenVLA achieve strong manipulation performance, but rely on millions of expert demonstrations and industrial-scale compute (e.g., TPU v4 Pods or large A100 clusters) trained for weeks. We introduce TAP (Task-Agnostic Pretraining), a two-stage framework that dramatically reduces both requirements. In Stage 1, we pretrain on task-agnostic interaction data (e.g., exploration or play) that is free in terms of human labeling cost. In Stage 2, we finetune with only a fraction of the expert demonstrations — as few as 200 real-world trajectories — yet achieve performance competitive with baselines trained on millions of trajectories [1]. Our full pipeline runs on a single node with 8×H100 GPUs in ~24 GPU hours, making the reproduction and iteration of VLA policies significantly more accessible to the academic community [1].

Key Contributions

Data Efficient

Reduces expert-data demand by several orders of magnitude, using only ~200 trajectories in real-world experiments while matching baselines trained on millions [1].

Academic-Scale Compute

Full pretraining + finetuning completes on a single 8×H100 node in ~24 GPU hours, versus weeks of industrial-scale training for typical VLAs [1].

Robust Generalization

Demonstrated on SIMPLER simulation (unlocking task-irrelevant data) and real-world unseen background shifts, showing robust manipulation policies [1].

Method & Results

Two-Stage Framework. Stage 1 pretrains on task-agnostic play/exploration data; Stage 2 finetunes with few expert demos.

Data Efficiency. TAP matches baselines using orders of magnitude fewer expert trajectories [1].

Simulation. Unlocking task-irrelevant data yields consistent gains on the SIMPLER benchmark [1].

Real-World Robustness. Strong performance under unseen background shifts [1].
